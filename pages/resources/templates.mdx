# CV templates

We recommended using **LaTeX** for creating CV files, but you can also use any text/document editor.

Here is the list of LaTeX templates:

1. [Deedy Resume](https://www.overleaf.com/latex/templates/deedy-resume/sqdbztjjghvz#.WaUd5CgjE2w)
2. [Curriculum Vitae](https://www.overleaf.com/latex/examples/curriculum-vitae/ztykfkztnqfh#.WaUeHigjE2w)
3. [CV for freshers](https://www.overleaf.com/latex/templates/cv-for-freshers/jkpwvnrdrxpm#.WaUeNygjE2w)
4. [Single column deedy CV](https://www.overleaf.com/latex/templates/single-column-deedy-cv-slash-resume-template/zwyxmkbrfgtz#.WaUeXygjE2w)

You can also use [this](https://www.overleaf.com/latex/templates/tagged/cv) big list of resume templates.

Also, **Career & Professional Development Center** of CMU has a [very good guide](https://www.cmu.edu/career/resource-library/resumes-and-cover-letters/index.html) on creating a resume.

Harvard univerysity also has [a guide](https://ocs.fas.harvard.edu/resumes-cvs-cover-letters).
